But  perhaps he never stopped to think that one might almost as well bite a  rock as his hard shell. And anybody might better chew a piece of leather  than try to take a mouthful out of his legs, or his neck, or his head.  So no one paid any heed to Timothy Turtle's kind offer. Even Peter Mink,  who was himself overfond of biting people, wisely let Mr. Turtle alone.  There is no doubt that it was the safer way.
