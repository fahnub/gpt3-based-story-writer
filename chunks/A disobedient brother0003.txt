In August it was a place of shady sweetness, fragrant  with the odour of ripening apples, full of dear, delicate shadows.  Through its openings we looked afar to the blue rims of the hills and  over green, old, tranquil fields, lying the sunset glow. Overhead the  lacing leaves made a green, murmurous roof.
