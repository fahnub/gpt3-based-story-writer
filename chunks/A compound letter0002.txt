Later, I, having at that time a mania for preserving all  documents relating to our life in Carlisle, copied those letters in the  blank pages at the back of my dream book. Hence I can reproduce them  verbatim, with the bouquet they have retained through all the long years  since they were penned in that autumnal orchard on the hill, with  its fading leaves and frosted grasses, and the "mild, delightsome  melancholy" of the late October day
