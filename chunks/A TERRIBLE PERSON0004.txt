And  there she would doze by the hour--especially after she had enjoyed a  hearty meal.  On summer nights, however, when she loved to hunt out of doors, Miss  Kitty Cat was far from appearing sleepy. She roamed about the fields, or  crept through the tree-tops with a stealthy tread and a tigerish working  of her tail. Folk smaller than Miss Kitty never cared to meet her at  such times. They knew that she would spring upon them if she had a  chance. So they took good care to keep out of her way.
