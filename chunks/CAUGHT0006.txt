It was the first time in his long life that he had ever found himself in  that position on land. And he was both frightened and angry.  Old Mr. Crow, who had a way of knowing when there was anything unusual  going on, arrived in time to hear Timothy's remarks. And what he said  about boys--and especially about Johnnie Green--made Mr. Crow catch his  breath.
