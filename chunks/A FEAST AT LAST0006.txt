By coming to that  spot early every day he could pick up buds enough--dropped carelessly by  Mr. Pine Finch--to feed himself until spring came and the snow melted  and uncovered the ground, where he knew he could find food.  So he went home and slept as he had not slept for weeks. And the next  morning, when he went back to the tree where he had found Mr. Pine  Finch, his eighteen cousins followed him.
