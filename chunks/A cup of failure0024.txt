Felix, Peter and I were all among the chiels who were taking  mental notes on the art of preaching a sermon. Not a motion, or glance,  or intonation escaped us. To be sure, none of us could remember the text  when we got home; but we knew just how you should throw back your head  and clutch the edge of the pulpit with both hands when you announced it.
