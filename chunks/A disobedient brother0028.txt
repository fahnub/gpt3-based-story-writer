Felicity could  have forgiven Uncle Roger anything but that. It really was unpardonable.  She confided to me as we climbed the stairs that she hated Uncle Roger.  Her red lips quivered and the tears of wounded pride brimmed over in  her beautiful blue eyes. In the dim candle-light she looked unbelievably  pretty and appealing. I put my arm about her and gave her a cousinly  salute.  "Never you mind him, Felicity," I said. "He's only a grown-up."
