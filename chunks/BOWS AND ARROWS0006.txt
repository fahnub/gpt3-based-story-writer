Trying to be as good and  gentle did not satisfy her; she must do something brave or surprising,  and no chance for distinguishing herself in that way seemed likely to  appear. Betty was as fond as ever, and the boys were very kind to her;  but she felt that they both liked "little Betcinda," as they called her,  best, because she found Sanch, and never seemed to know that she had  done any thing brave in defending him against all odds.
