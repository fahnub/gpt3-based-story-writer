Of Ben Brown the elder's  death there was little to tell, except that he was killed in some wild  place at the West, and a stranger wrote the fact to the only person  whose name was found in Ben's pocket-book. Mr. Smithers offered to take  the boy back and "do well by him," averring that the father wished his  son to remain where he left him, and follow the profession to which he  was trained.
