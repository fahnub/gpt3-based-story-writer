And when the eggs were laid at last, all his leaves stroked each other  for joy, and the noise they made was so sweet that the mother-tree bent  over to see why he was so happy.  The mother-bird sat patiently on the nest all day, and when, now and  then, she flew away to rest her tired little legs, the father-bird came  to keep the eggs warm.
