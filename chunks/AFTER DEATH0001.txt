AFTER DEATH (Poetry:- Ages 3 to 5)      IF we must part, this parting is the best:    How would you bear to lay    Your head on some warm pillow far away--    Your head, so used to lying on my breast?      But now your pillow is cold;    Your hands have flowers, and not my hands, to hold;    Upon our bed the worn bride-linen lies.
