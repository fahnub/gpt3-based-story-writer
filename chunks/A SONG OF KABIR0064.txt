"Be still, be still! It is  the fault of the night, and no fault of thine."  "It was the smells of the night," said Bagheera penitently. "This air  cries aloud to me. But how dost THOU know?"  Of course the air round an Indian village is full of all kinds of  smells, and to any creature who does nearly all his thinking through his  nose, smells are as maddening as music and drugs are to human beings.
