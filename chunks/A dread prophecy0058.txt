When we went to bed it was settling down for  a stormy night, and the rain was streaming wetly on the roof, as if the  world, like Sara Ray, were weeping because its end was so near. Nobody  forgot or hurried over his prayers that night. We would dearly have  loved to leave the candle burning, but Aunt Janet's decree regarding  this was as inexorable as any of Mede and Persia.
