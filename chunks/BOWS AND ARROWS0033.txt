Every one enjoyed the new play very much, and something grew out of it  which was a lasting pleasure to many, long after the bows and arrows  were forgotten. Seeing how glad the children were to get a new story,  Miss Celia was moved to send a box of books--old and new--to the  town library, which was but scantily supplied, as country libraries are  apt to be.
