Curdie and His Mother (Fiction:- Ages 5 to 10)  Curdie went up the mountain neither whistling nor singing, for he was  vexed with Irene for taking him in, as he called it; and he was vexed  with himself for having spoken to her so angrily.  His mother gave a  cry of joy when she saw him, and at once set about getting him  something to eat, asking him questions all the time, which he did not  answer so cheerfully as usual.  When his meal was ready, she left him  to eat it, and hurried to the mine
