And the thinly clad, shivering knot  of field folk sat themselves down unhappily and waited for the tailor to  appear. Every one of them wanted a warm new overcoat, for each expected  to be out late that night.  Meanwhile Mr. Frog watched them--and giggled as loud as he dared. It was  Mr. Crow's committee that thronged about his door--the people who were  expecting to call upon Kiddie Katydid that very night to invite him to  join the Pleasant Valley orchestra.
