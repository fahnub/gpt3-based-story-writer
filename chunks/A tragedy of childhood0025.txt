They were wonderfully dainty, with a strange, faint,  aromatic perfume which was only to be detected at a little distance and  vanished if you bent over them. They faded soon after they were plucked;  and, although strangers, greatly admiring them, often carried away roots  and seeds, they could never be coaxed to grow elsewhere.
