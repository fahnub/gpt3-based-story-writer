As it was, his face was very dirty. But he never went back to his  mother's house until he had washed it carefully, just as a cat washes her  face.  Sometimes Sandy found stones in his way, down there beneath the pasture.  And those he had to push up, too. Sometimes a stone was too big to crowd  through the opening into the world outside. And then Sandy had to make  the opening bigger. After he had done that, and pushed the stone out upon  his dirt-pile, he would make his doorway smaller again
