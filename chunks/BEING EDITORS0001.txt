BEING EDITORS (Fiction:- Ages 5 to 9)  It was Albert's uncle who thought of our trying a newspaper. He said he  thought we should not find the bandit business a paying industry, as a  permanency, and that journalism might be.  We had sold Noel's poetry and that piece of information about Lord  Tottenham to the good editor, so we thought it would not be a bad idea  to have a newspaper of our own. We saw plainly that editors must be very  rich and powerful, because of the grand office and the man
