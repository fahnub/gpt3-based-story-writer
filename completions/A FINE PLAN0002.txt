He knew that after sunset almost all the birds were  asleep--except for Solomon Owl and Willie Whip-poor-will and a few other  feathered folk who preferred the dark to the daylight. They were not so  numerous that they worried Chirpy very much. But between dawn and sunset  there were altogether too many birds awake to please him. Then Chirpy  Cricket kept quite silent.
