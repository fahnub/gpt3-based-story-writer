Once more Mowgli stared, as he had stared at the  rebellious cubs, full into the beryl-green eyes till the red glare  behind their green went out like the light of a lighthouse shut off  twenty miles across the sea; till the eyes dropped, and the big head  with them--dropped lower and lower, and the red rasp of a tongue grated  on Mowgli's instep.  "Brother--Brother--Brother!" the boy whispered, stroking steadily and  lightly from the neck along the heaving back.
