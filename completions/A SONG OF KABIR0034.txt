imagine what it  sounds like when it breaks the afternoon hush of the Jungle:--     One moment past our bodies cast     No shadow on the plain;     Now clear and black they stride our track,     And we run home again.     In morning hush, each rock and bush     Stands hard, and high, and raw:     Then give the Call: "Good rest to all     That keep The Jungle Law!"     Now horn and pelt our peoples melt     In covert to abide;     Now, crouched and still, to cave and hill     Our Jungle Barons
