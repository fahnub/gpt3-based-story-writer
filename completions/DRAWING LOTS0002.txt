Usually the honey-makers began to grow very drowsy at that time of day  (it was then late in the afternoon). But now they were so angry that  they were not the least bit sleepy. Their own buzzing kept them awake.  And the Queen was glad that it was so, because she herself never could  have stopped so many of them from going to sleep.
