'I can't think how you do it, mother,' he would say.  'I follow the thread,' she would answer--'just as you do in the mine.'  She never had more to say about it; but the less clever she was with  her words, the more clever she was with her hands; and the less his  mother said, the more Curdie believed she had to say.
