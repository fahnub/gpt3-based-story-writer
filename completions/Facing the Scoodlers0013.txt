"Don't want to be soup!" wailed Button-Bright again; and Toto began to  whine dismally, as if he didn't want to be soup, either.  "The only thing to do," said the shaggy man to his friends, in a low  tone, "is to get out of this pocket in the rocks as soon as we can, and  leave the Scoodlers behind us.  Follow me, my dears, and don't pay any  attention to what they do or say."
