But it was evident that his sufferings were not  forgotten; his once sweet temper was a trifle soured; and, with a few  exceptions, he had lost his faith in mankind. Before, he had been the  most benevolent and hospitable of dogs; now, he eyed all strangers  suspiciously, and the sight of a shabby man made him growl and bristle  up, as if the memory of his wrongs still burned hotly within him.
