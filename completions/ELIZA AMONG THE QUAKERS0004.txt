Eliza, 'to-night!'  Then it seemed as if the room and everything in it swam round her, and she fell into Rachel's arms.  Very gently Rachel laid her down on the bed. Eliza slept as she had not slept since the dreadful night when she had taken her boy and run away through the cold, dark night.  She dreamed of a beautiful country--a land, it seemed to her, of rest--green shores, pleasant islands, and lovely glittering water.
