Much scared, he tried to wade out, but  could only flounder to a tussock of grass, and cling there, while he  endeavored to kick his legs free. He got them out, but struggled in vain  to coil them up or to hoist his heavy body upon the very small island in  this sea of mud. Down they splashed again; and Sam gave a dismal groan  as he thought of the leeches and water-snakes which might be lying in  wait below.
