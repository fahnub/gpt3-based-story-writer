The mother-tree had gone,--"gone to be useful," said the little Fir; and  though he missed her very much, and the world seemed very empty when he  looked up and no longer saw her thick branches and her strong trunk, yet  he was not unhappy, for he was a brave little Fir.  Still the days grew colder, and often the Fir-tree wondered if the  children who had made a ring and danced about him would remember him  when Christmas time came.
