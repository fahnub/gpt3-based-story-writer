Few people passed along the lane, and the sun was setting, so the  prospect of a night in the marsh nerved Sam to make a frantic plunge  toward the bulrush island, which was nearer than the mainland, and  looked firmer than any tussock round him. But he failed to reach this  haven of rest, and was forced to stop at an old stump which stuck up,  looking very like the moss-grown horns of the "dear departed."
