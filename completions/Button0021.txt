Cap'n Bill made no reply to this statement, which showed he did not  fully agree with the little girl; but he fell into deep thought, with  wrinkled brows, and finally he said:  "If those purple berries would make anything grow bigger, whether it'd  eaten the lavender ones or not, I could find a way out of our troubles."  They did not understand this speech and looked at the old sailor as if  expecting him to explain what he meant. But just then a chorus of  shrill cries rose from outside.
