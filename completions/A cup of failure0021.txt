I have that sermon still, all its red thumps unfaded, lying beside my  dream book; but I am not going to inflict it on my readers. I am not so  proud of it as I once was. I was really puffed up with earthly vanity  over it at that time. Felix, I thought, would be hard put to it to beat  it. As for Peter, I did not consider him a rival to be feared.
