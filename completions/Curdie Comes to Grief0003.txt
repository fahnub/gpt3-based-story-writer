She kept foolishly  whispering to the servants, however--sometimes that the princess was  not right in her mind, sometimes that she was too good to live, and  other nonsense of the same sort.  All this time Curdie had to be sorry, without a chance of confessing,  that he had behaved so unkindly to the princess.  This perhaps made him  the more diligent in his endeavours to serve her.
